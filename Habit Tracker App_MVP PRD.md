# Habit Tracker App - MVP PRD

## 1. Product Overview

This product is a habit tracker mobile app designed not just for logging habits, but for helping users **sustain behaviors over time**.

The core philosophy:
> Users don’t fail to start habits — they fail to maintain them.

---

## 2. Market & User Psychology Insights

### Market Reality
- Habit tracker market is saturated
- High initial engagement, low long-term retention
- Core problem = **lack of sustained usage**

### Key Psychological Drivers

1. Instant Gratification
   - Users respond to immediate rewards

2. Sense of Progress
   - Visual feedback increases motivation

3. Identity Formation
   - “I am a person who does X”

4. Loss Aversion
   - Users fear breaking streaks

---

## 3. User Personas

### Persona 1: Sarah (Stressed College Student)
- Goal: Maintain consistent study habits
- Pain: Gives up after failure, inconsistent schedule
- Motivation: Progress visibility, streaks

### Persona 2: Michael (Busy Professional)
- Goal: Maintain minimal self-discipline routines
- Pain: No time, decision fatigue
- Motivation: Simplicity, quick execution

### Persona 3: David (Data-driven Fitness User)
- Goal: Optimize performance through data
- Pain: Lack of insights, fragmented data
- Motivation: Analytics, measurable improvement

---

## 4. Product Objectives (MVP)

### Problem Statement
Users struggle with:
- Lack of consistency
- High friction in execution
- Low visibility of progress
- Weak motivation systems

### Target Users (MVP Focus)
Primary:
- Sarah (students)
- Michael (busy professionals)

Secondary:
- David (future expansion)

### Core Value Proposition

> “Make habits easy to continue, even after failure.”

Key pillars:
- Frictionless execution
- Recovery-friendly system
- Instant feedback
- Simple progress visibility

---

## 5. Core Functionalities (MVP)

- Habit creation & management
- Habit completion tracking
- Daily reminders
- Basic progress tracking (streak, completion rate)

---

## 6. User Stories

### Michael

- Create habits quickly without complexity
- Record habits in 1–2 seconds
- Receive simple, actionable reminders
- Check daily completion at a glance

### Sarah

- Create flexible study habits
- Record partial success (not all-or-nothing)
- Receive gentle reminders
- Track progress without losing motivation after failure

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Features (MVP)

### Excluded Features

1. Advanced analytics
2. AI-based personalization
3. Social/community features
4. Gamification systems (levels, items)
5. Device integrations
6. Advanced customization
7. Coaching/content systems

### Guiding Principle

> If the feature is not required for daily usage → exclude from MVP

---

## 8. Success Metrics (MVP)

### 1. Retention Rate (D7 / D30)
- Measures sustained usage

### 2. Habit Completion Rate
- Measures actual behavior change

### 3. Daily Active Usage (with habit check)
- Measures engagement quality

### Example Targets

- D7 Retention: 30–40%
- Completion Rate: >60%
- Active usage: ≥4 days/week

---

## 9. Key Product Principle

> This is not a “smart habit app”
> It is a “system that prevents users from quitting”
